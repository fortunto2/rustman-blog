Hard numeric thresholds for weekly product decisions. No "let's wait and see":

| Week | Metric | KILL | ITERATE | SCALE |
|------|--------|------|---------|-------|
| 1 | Fake-Door clicks | < 10 per $20 | — | — |
| 2 | Signups | 0 | 1-4 | ≥ 5 |
| 3+ | Growth WoW | < 0% | 0-50% | > 50% |

**ARCHIVE** (soft kill): declining signups for 2+ consecutive weeks.
**SCALE** actions: new locale, partnerships, programmatic SEO.

Core value: **predetermined numbers remove emotional attachment.** When the kill threshold is set before launch — data makes the decision, not ego.

This is the antidote to sunk cost fallacy. Spent 3 days — signups 0 — KILL. Not "maybe tweak the marketing," but KILL.
