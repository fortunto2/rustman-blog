## Key Claims

The manifesto (86 lines) establishes 6 foundational positions:

1. **Revenue = feedback, not the goal.** Each product is an experiment, a question to the world. Money shows whether the market answered.

2. **Privacy is architecture, not a feature.** On-device, local storage, no accounts. If we can't see your data, we can't leak it.

3. **Anti-exploitation.** No dark patterns, no addiction mechanics, no discrimination. Software serves people.

4. **Against subscription fatigue.** Not everything needs a monthly fee. One-time purchase or honest pricing. Against lock-in.

5. **One pain → one feature → launch.** Not platforms, not roadmaps. One problem, one solution, shipped in days. Doesn't work — kill.

6. **Antifragile architecture.** Modular, no single points of failure. Barbell: small bets with asymmetric upside.
